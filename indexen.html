Academic Profile

Zeydan Çalışkan is an undergraduate student in Software Engineering at the International Cyprus University. His academic interests include emotional intelligence, human-computer interaction, and cultural psychology, where he adopts interdisciplinary approaches. Through his research, he aims to contribute to human-centered design and digital interaction by exploring how technological systems influence individuals’ cognitive and emotional processes.

He is also deeply interested in the relationships between language, culture, and belief, as well as cultural interactions. In addition to his software engineering background, he develops a comprehensive perspective in anthropology, biology, and psychology.
